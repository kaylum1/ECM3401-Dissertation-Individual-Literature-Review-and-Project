Fingerprinting Scan - Understanding Browser Fingerprinting

This page will break down and explains fingerprinting scans, what it means when a scan does not detect any fingerprinting mechanisms, how users and organisations can be impacted by invasive fingerprinting techniques, and the process behind these scans.

What is a Fingerprinting Scan?

A fingerprinting scan is an inspection that uncovers ways a site or app harvests distinctive details about a visitor’s device or browser. Information such as browser version, operating system, screen size, installed fonts, and plugins can be combined to form a unique “digital fingerprint.”

Key aspects of a fingerprinting scan include some of the following below:

  • Data Collection Analysis: Identifying the types of device and browser information being gathered.
  • Method Detection: Detecting techniques such as canvas or WebGL fingerprinting.
  • Privacy Impact Evaluation: This will assess how the collected data could be used to track or profile users.

What Happens When a Fingerprinting Scan Shows Nothing?

If a fingerprinting scan does not detect any fingerprinting mechanisms, it may indicate:

  • That the website or application does not implement any active fingerprinting techniques.
  • Could be that any fingerprinting methods in use are effectively minimised or masked to protect user privacy.
  • The scan might not capture subtle or emerging fingerprinting strategies.
  • Regular monitoring is advised as new fingerprinting methods can emerge over time.

A clean scan result is positive, but ongoing vigilance is essential to maintain user privacy.

How Users and Organizations are Exposed to Fingerprinting Risks

Absent strong controls, fingerprinting can lead to:

  • Privacy Invasion: Detailed tracking of browsing habits without consent.
  • Targeted Advertising: Personalised ads driven by unique user profiles.
  • Data Profiling: Aggregated data enabling fine-grained analysis of behaviour.
  • Reduced Anonymity: Difficulty staying private across multiple sessions.

How to Mitigate Fingerprinting Risks

To protect user privacy and mitigate fingerprinting risks, consider these best practices:

  • Minimise Data Exposure: By limiting the amount of device and browser information shared with websites.
  • Implement Anti-Fingerprinting Measures: Use various techniques such as browser spoofing or randomisation to obscure unique identifiers.
  • Educate Users: By Informing users about fingerprinting and provide tools or settings to enhance privacy when online.
  • Regular Audits: Continuously scan for fingerprinting mechanisms and update privacy practices accordingly.

The Science Behind Fingerprinting Scanning

Fingerprinting scanners operate by simulating different user environments and collecting the data points that websites can access. The process includes:

  • Simulating Diverse Conditions: Visiting the site with various browser and device configurations.
  • Aggregating Data Points: Recording values like resolution, fonts, and plugins.
  • Pattern Analysis: Judging whether the combined data can single out an individual.
  • Reporting Results: Highlighting fingerprinting vectors and suggesting defences.

Why Fingerprinting Protection is Essential

Robust protection against fingerprinting matters because it:

  • Preserves Privacy: Stops unwanted tracking and keeps users anonymous.
  • Builds Trust: Demonstrates a respect for user privacy, boosting confidence.
  • Enables Compliance: Helps meet data-protection laws and regulations.
  • Supports Ethical Data Use: Promotes transparency and fairness in data collection
